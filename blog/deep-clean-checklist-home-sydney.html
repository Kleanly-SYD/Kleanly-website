The Ultimate Deep Clean Checklist for Your Home

20 March 2026
9 min read
By Kleanly

A regular clean keeps a home tidy. A deep clean restores it. The difference lies in all the places a regular clean skips — inside appliances, behind furniture, under sink traps, on top of cabinets, inside window tracks. Over time, these areas accumulate grime, bacteria, and allergens that affect the air quality and feel of your home.

This checklist covers every area of your home, room by room. Work through it once or twice a year, or book a professional deep clean and hand it over entirely.

How long does it take? A thorough deep clean of an average 2-bedroom home takes 4–6 hours for one person, or 2–3 hours for a team of two. Set aside a full day and work methodically — rushing leads to missed areas.

Kitchen

The kitchen collects more grease, bacteria, and odour than any other room. Don't skip anything here.

🍳 Kitchen Checklist

  • Clean inside the oven — remove racks, soak in hot soapy water, apply oven cleaner to cavity
  • Degrease stovetop burners, drip trays, and surrounding surfaces
  • Clean the rangehood and soak the grease filter
  • Wipe inside the microwave — use a bowl of hot water with lemon to loosen dried food first
  • Empty, defrost (if needed), and wipe inside the fridge and freezer
  • Pull fridge and oven out — clean underneath and behind
  • Wipe down all cabinet doors and handles inside and out
  • Empty and wipe all pantry shelves and drawers
  • Descale the kettle and clean the coffee machine
  • Clean the dishwasher — run an empty cycle with a dishwasher cleaner tablet
  • Scrub the sink and descale the tap
  • Clean the splashback — remove grease buildup with a degreaser
  • Wipe the tops of all cabinets — these are dust and grease traps
  • Clean light fittings and rangehood exterior
  • Mop floor including under the fridge and along skirting boards

Bathrooms & Toilets

Bathrooms need particular attention to mould, limescale, and soap scum — all of which build up over time and become harder to remove if left too long.

🚿 Bathroom Checklist

  • Scrub and descale the shower — tiles, grout, screen, and floor
  • Treat any mould on grout with a bleach-based spray
  • Remove limescale from taps, showerhead, and bath spout
  • Clean the toilet inside the bowl, under the rim, the seat, base, and behind the cistern
  • Wipe down vanity inside and out, including under the sink
  • Clean the mirror until streak-free
  • Clean exhaust fan cover — remove and wash if possible
  • Wipe down tiles and walls
  • Clean the shower drain — remove hair and debris from the trap
  • Wipe skirting boards and door frames
  • Mop floor and clean behind the toilet

Bedrooms

🛏 Bedroom Checklist

  • Wash all bedding including pillows, doonas, and mattress protectors
  • Vacuum the mattress and flip or rotate if possible
  • Wipe down all furniture surfaces including tops, sides, and underneath
  • Empty and wipe inside wardrobes — shelves, rails, and drawer interiors
  • Clean ceiling fan blades and light fittings
  • Wipe window sills, tracks, and frames
  • Clean fly screens if present
  • Wipe skirting boards and door frames
  • Vacuum under and behind the bed and furniture
  • Vacuum or steam clean carpet; mop hard floors

Living & Dining Areas

🛋 Living & Dining Checklist

  • Dust all surfaces with a damp microfibre cloth — including shelving, entertainment units, and picture frames
  • Vacuum and spot-clean upholstered sofas and chairs
  • Clean under cushions — remove and vacuum beneath them
  • Wipe down the TV and any electronics (dust accumulates heavily here)
  • Clean light fittings, lampshades, and switches
  • Wipe all windows and sliding doors inside
  • Clean blinds — wipe each slat with a damp cloth or use a blind duster
  • Dust curtain rails or wipe curtain rods
  • Wipe skirting boards and door frames
  • Vacuum under furniture and along walls
  • Mop or vacuum floors

Laundry

🧺 Laundry Checklist

  • Clean inside the washing machine drum — run a hot empty cycle with white vinegar or a washing machine cleaner
  • Wipe the detergent drawer and door seal (rubber gasket traps mould)
  • Clean the dryer lint filter and wipe inside the drum
  • Pull both appliances out — clean behind and underneath
  • Clean the laundry tub and descale the tap
  • Wipe shelving and cabinet interiors
  • Clean the walls and skirting boards
  • Mop the floor

Entry, Hallway & Stairs

🚪 Entry & Hallway Checklist

  • Wipe down the front door inside and out — including the door frame and handle
  • Clean light fittings and switches
  • Wipe skirting boards along the full length of the hallway
  • Clean any mirrors or artwork
  • If stairs: vacuum each step and wipe the banister and spindles
  • Vacuum or mop floors

The Areas Most People Forget

Even diligent cleaners often overlook these spots — they make a big difference when addressed:

  • Top of the refrigerator — collects a thick layer of greasy dust
  • Inside light switches and power point covers — wipe the surrounds
  • Door tops and above door frames — dust accumulates here unseen
  • Under and behind the toilet — often skipped during regular cleans
  • Window tracks — fill with debris and dead insects
  • Under the kitchen sink — check for leaks and wipe down the cabinet interior
  • Air conditioning filters — remove and wash or vacuum according to the manufacturer's guide
  • Ceiling corners — cobwebs accumulate here, especially in corners and behind doors
How often should you deep clean? Most homes benefit from a full deep clean every 6–12 months, in addition to regular weekly cleaning. If you have pets, young children, or allergies, more frequent deep cleans make a noticeable difference to air quality and hygiene.
